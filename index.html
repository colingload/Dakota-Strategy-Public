What is SDE? What is EBITDA?

SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings)

The total financial benefit to a single owner-operator. Includes the owner's salary, perks, and one-time expenses added back to net income. The standard valuation metric for businesses where the owner is the business.

EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation & Amortization)

Operating profit independent of any single person. Measures what the business earns on its own, regardless of who owns it or how it's financed.

The practical difference: SDE assumes one owner will replace another. EBITDA assumes the business runs without any specific individual. That distinction is what changes the multiple — and the buyer pool.

The Recycle

Profit interest earned by Dakota flows up into a holding company (holdco). Upon exit, the departing owner receives a stake in the holdco — creating two things:

Continuation

The owner maintains ongoing participation in a diversified, growing portfolio. They don't walk away — they step into a broader version of it.

Long-term vested interest

The owner has skin in the game for business operations to succeed post-transition. Incentives stay aligned well beyond the close.
